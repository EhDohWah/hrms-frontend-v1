  inflated tax deductions, potentially zeroing out tax for employees who should
  be taxed:

  Bug 1: Phantom SSF deduction ‚ÄĒ calculateSocialSecurityFromSalary() (line 533)
  calculates SSF for ALL employees, including Expats who don't actually pay SSF.

  Bug 2: Phantom PVD deduction ‚ÄĒ calculateProvidentFundFromEmployeeData() (line 
  552) applies PVD at 7.5% of annual income for ALL Local ID employees,
  regardless of whether they're enrolled in PVD or have passed probation.

  The payroll correctly checks (line 1186-1212):
  - employment.pvd toggle must be enabled
  - Probation must be passed
  - Employee status must match

  But the TaxCalculationService ignores all of this and uses theoretical
  maximums, inflating deductions and reducing taxable income below the 150k
  threshold.

  Example: If 0094 is "Local ID Staff" at ~30-35k salary:
  - Phantom PVD: 330k-385k √ó 7.5% = 24,750-28,875 extra deduction
  - This pushes taxable income below 150k ‚Üí tax = 0
  - Without phantom PVD, taxable income would be ~160k ‚Üí tax > 0

  Let me fix this by passing actual payroll SSF and PVD values to the tax
  calculation.
